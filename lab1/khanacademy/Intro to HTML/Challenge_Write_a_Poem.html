The Silver Bridge

The moon retreats on velvet feet,
To let the golden arrows fly,
Where indigo and amber meet,
Across the canvas of the sky.

The world is held in crystal hush,
Before the city starts to ring,
Within the morning's rosy blush,
The silent birds begin to sing.

The dew still clings to emerald blades,
A fleeting gift the darkness gave,
As every ghostly shadow fades,
Beneath the light’s arriving wave.
